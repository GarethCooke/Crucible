## Lessons from demos 5 and 6 applied to this plan

- **§1 exists at all.** Demo 6's pilot reframed the headline mid-flight; demo 7's headline ("most maps are too small for the hash map to win") is similarly contingent on where the crossover sits. If the pilot shows N_crossover outside the 16–1024 range, the thesis reframes before §2 commits.
- **TimeVsN reuse rather than fork.** Demo 6 extended `<TimeVsN>` with cache-tier band markers; demo 7 inherits that work directly. §5 is mostly verification plus a small axis-name extension. Resist forking a "MapCrossover" component — extend if needed, fork only if extension breaks DRY badly.
- **Capture environment in §2's brief explicitly.** Headless. Stated, not assumed.
- **`sudo cset shield --reset` is a precondition.** Now in `BRIEF.md` (hoisted at demo 6 close-out). Named in §2's brief and §6's capture checklist as a reminder; not duplicated as a methodology disclosure on the post itself — the isolation outcome has been consistent across demos 1–7, and surfacing the procedural tightening on-page would manufacture a confound that doesn't exist in the data.
- **No backlog brief-format change.** Demo 6's Phase 2 shape worked. Keeping it.

## Open items (to be resolved during §1 / §2)

- **N upper bound.** Likely 10⁶ or 10⁷. Pilot decides — upper bound should sit well past where the hash map decisively wins, but not so far that the chart's small-N region (where the story lives) gets visually crushed by log-scale compression.
- **`std::map` on the headline chart, or relegated to a sidebar callout?** It'll lose everywhere; whether it earns a line on chart 1 or sits in a paragraph is an editorial call. Default: include on the headline; the flat line above everything reinforces "don't reach for this by default."
- **Workload-mix split for chart 3.** Pure-lookup as headline (config-map framing); supporting sweep at 10% / 50% / 90% inserts. Confirm the lookup-heavy assumption in pilot.
- **Key distribution.** Random vs sequential. Random is harder on sorted-vector `lower_bound` (no prefetch help) and more realistic. Default: random; document.
- **`std::flat_map` implementation availability.** Confirm libstdc++ at the toolchain version ships a usable `std::flat_map`. If not, fall back to `boost::flat_map` and call out the substitution in the post. Pilot answers this before §2 — and the answer changes the brief's implementation list materially.
- **`std::map` and `std::vector<pair>+lower_bound` as separate lines, or fold the primitive into the `std::flat_map` line?** The primitive is purer (no library overhead); `std::flat_map` is what people reach for. Keeping both surfaces whatever overhead the library adds; merging them simplifies the chart. Editorial; default to keeping both for the first capture, then dropping the primitive if the lines overlap.
